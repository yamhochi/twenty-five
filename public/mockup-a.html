Context: the company and my role

CreditorWatch is an Australian B2B credit risk intelligence platform. Its core value proposition: help businesses monitor customers and debtors for financial risk before it hits the bottom line.

When I joined, the platform was at an inflection point. The core product represented ~70% of revenue, but it was showing cracks. Churn was rising, competitive pressure was mounting, and the internal strategy was doubling down on high-level portfolio reporting — an expensive bet I was brought in to pressure-test.

"Too much noise, not enough signal."

— Recurring theme across user interviews. Users weren't asking for fewer alerts. They were asking for clearer meaning.

The business challenge

—

Churn was rising as customers questioned whether the value justified the cost

—

The data model was at risk. Dormant users don't share data. Disengagement was a structural threat, not just a UX problem.

—

Repositioning had its challenges. The market still saw CreditorWatch as a data provider despite expanding capabilities.

My approach: diagnose before build

Dashboard audit findings and behavioural data

Rather than diving straight into solutioning, I ran a structured discovery program to establish a baseline and stress-test the hypothesis.

Jobs-to-be-Done · Three-Stage Mental Model

01 · Awareness

Is this alert worth my attention?

02 · Investigation

How does this risk affect my actual exposure?

03 · Actioning

What do I do now, and can I do it here?

The critical insight: the business assumed users complained about too many alerts. The data showed they were complaining about a lack of synthesis.
