CreditorWatch · 2022–2024

From data dump to AI-assisted decisioning engine

A 2-year platform transformation at CreditorWatch — turning a fragmented credit risk tool into a cohesive decisioning engine while sustaining 30% YoY revenue growth through a major price increase.

01 / Context

The company and my role

CreditorWatch is an Australian B2B credit risk intelligence platform. When I joined, the core product — credit reports and alerting — represented ~70% of revenue but was showing cracks. Churn was rising, competitive pressure was mounting, and the internal bet on high-level portfolio reporting was one I was brought in to pressure-test.

02 / Challenge

The business was straining

Growth had been driven by customer acquisition and annual price increases. But the model was beginning to break: churn was rising as customers questioned the value, and the data model underpinning the platform's moat — data reciprocity — was at risk. Dormant users don't share data.

"Too much noise, not enough signal."

Users weren't asking for fewer alerts — they were asking for clearer meaning.

03 / Approach

Diagnose before build

Dashboard audit findings and behavioural data — the evidence base for the strategic pivot.

Rather than solutioning, I ran a structured discovery program. Behavioural data showed one-off lookups and PDF downloads at all-time highs — but everything else was disproportionately under-used. Alert email click-through sat at 4%.

Pillar 2 — Investigation

Before

After

Pillar 3 — Actioning

Rather than forcing daily engagement, we redesigned the Dashboard as a Status Engine — surfacing "what's changed since you last logged in" in a snackable weekly check-in format.

05 / What comes next

AI readiness

The last two years structured the data, categorised risk events, mapped user appetite, and built contextual investigation logic. The platform is now more AI-ready than it has ever been.
